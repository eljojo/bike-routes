The contents of this guide are based on what I learned after attending [Dave Shellnutt, The Bike Lawyer](https://www.thebikinglawyer.ca)'s workshop and [reading their blog](https://www.thebikinglawyer.ca/post/the-biking-lawyer-s-crash-guide).

During the workshop, Dave talked about the importance of reporting bike crashes and gave us a [Crash Report Card](https://www.thebikinglawyer.ca/post/bike-crash-reports-mapping).

The card links to the [Crash Report Form](https://www.thebikinglawyer.ca/crash-report), and mentions the following things to write down in case of accident:

- Date, time, and crash location
- License Plate Number
- Driver's License Number
- Driver Insurance Company & Policy Number
- Witnesses / Contact Info
- Police Badge / Occurrence Number
- Take photos of the crash scene and vehicles

**Seek medical attention via ambulance or walk in**. Medical bills and other adjacent costs [**will likely be paid for**](https://www.thebikinglawyer.ca/post/2019-06-18-hit-by-a-car-how-to-access-free-health-benefits-and-lost-wages) [by insurance](https://www.health.gov.on.ca/en/public/publications/ohip/motorvehicle.aspx).
