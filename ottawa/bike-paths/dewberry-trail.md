A 1.2-km boardwalk loop through the Mer Bleue bog, one of the most unusual ecosystems in southern Ontario — a northern peat bog hanging on thousands of kilometres south of where it belongs, surviving on cold air pooled in the old Ottawa River channel. Black spruce, tamarack, bog cranberry, pitcher plants. In migration season it's a warbler hotspot.

The path is an NCC-maintained boardwalk, not a road surface — walking pace, not a destination to ride fast through. Bring binoculars.

More info on the [NCC website](https://ncc-ccn.gc.ca/places/dewberry-trail).
