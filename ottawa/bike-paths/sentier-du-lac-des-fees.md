A paved pathway looping around Lac des Fées — a smaller, quieter lake tucked into the residential neighbourhoods just west of Hull. The roughly 4.7 km loop offers a peaceful ride with water views and is popular with local families.

The lake was known as Fairy Lake on early English-language maps, and the name was [officially adopted as Lac des Fées](https://toponymie.gouv.qc.ca/ct/ToposWeb/Fiche.aspx?no_seq=22143) by Quebec's Commission de toponymie in 1970. The name traces back to an [Algonquin legend](https://www.reseaupatrimoine.ca/sur-les-traces-du-patrimoine/fiches/lac-des-fees/): a young woman courted by two warriors threw herself into the water after both were killed in battle, and locals claimed to see a figure dressed in white near the surface — a "fairy" of the lake.

In the 1920s the slopes around the lake drew crowds for a different reason — the Cliffside Ski Club held ski-jumping competitions here, making it one of the area's early recreational hubs.

Connected to the broader Gatineau pathway network, it's an easy detour from the main east-west corridors if you want a change of pace. The lake itself is pretty in any season, but especially when the leaves turn.
