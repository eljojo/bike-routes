The Renfrew County section of the [Ottawa Valley Recreational Trail](/paths/ottawa-valley-recreation-trail), running 219 kilometres from Arnprior through Pembroke and onward to the Township of Papineau-Cameron border, through 10 municipalities. When combined with the Lanark County section to the south, the full corridor stretches [296 km from near Smiths Falls to Mattawa](https://ontariobiketrails.com/item/ottawa-valley-rail-trail/) — the longest rail trail in Ontario.

The trail follows the old [CP Rail Chalk River subdivision](https://millstonenews.com/ottawa-valley-rail-lines-being-torn-up/), which once carried freight and VIA Rail's transcontinental _Canadian_ through the Ottawa Valley. The line lost its economic base as pulp, paper, and manufacturing industries declined in the region. Rails between Renfrew and Pembroke were [removed in 2011](https://www.trainorders.com/discussion/read.php?15%2C2533566=). In [2016, the County of Renfrew, Lanark County, and the Township of Papineau-Cameron purchased the 300-kilometre corridor](https://www.pembroketoday.ca/2023/01/11/county-of-renfrew-will-continue-work-on-algonquin-trail/) and began converting it to trail.

The surface is limestone stone dust — rideable on 35mm tires or wider. As of 2023, [110 km from Arnprior to Petawawa is complete](https://www.pembroketoday.ca/2023/01/11/county-of-renfrew-will-continue-work-on-algonquin-trail/), with the next 53 km through Garrison Petawawa to Chalk River under development. The county received roughly 100,000 tonnes of limestone from the Ministry of Transportation for surfacing, stockpiled at Chalk River and Deux-Rivières.

The trail is part of the [Trans Canada Trail](https://www.countyofrenfrew.on.ca/en/news/algonquin-trail-joins-trans-canada-trail.aspx) and connects to the [Cycloparc PPJ](/paths/cycloparc-ppj) across the river in Quebec.
