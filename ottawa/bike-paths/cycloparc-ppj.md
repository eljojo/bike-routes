A 92-km rail trail on the Quebec side, running through the Pontiac region of the Outaouais northwest of Gatineau. The PPJ — named after the old Pontiac Pacific Junction Railway — offers long stretches of crushed stone trail through rural Quebec countryside, from Wyman in the east to Allumette Island in the west.

The [original railway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pontiac_Pacific_Junction_Railway) was incorporated in 1880 and ran from Aylmer through the Pontiac to Pembroke, Ontario. Locals nicknamed it the ["Push, Pull and Jerk."](https://www.theequity.ca/the-push-pull-and-jerk/) Passenger service ended in 1959, freight followed in the 1980s, and the rails were dismantled in 1984. The MRC Pontiac purchased the corridor and converted it into a recreational trail, which officially opened on May 12, 2008. Along the way the trail crosses the [Félix-Gabriel-Marchand covered bridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/F%C3%A9lix-Gabriel-Marchand_Bridge) — the second longest operational covered bridge in Canada.

The trail is part of Route Verte, Quebec's provincial cycling network. It passes through Bristol, Shawville, Campbell's Bay, Fort-Coulonge, and L'Isle-aux-Allumettes, with slopes never exceeding 4%. It's quieter and more remote than the Ottawa-side trails — you might be the only cyclist all afternoon. The flat, rock-dust surface makes it accessible to all types of riders, and six secondary circuits branch off the main trail for those who want to explore further. Good for a day trip or a longer bikepacking adventure into the Pontiac region.
