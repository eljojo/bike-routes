Route 1 of the [Route Verte](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Route_verte), Quebec's provincial cycling network. It runs from Gatineau eastward through Montréal, Sherbrooke, Lévis, Rivière-du-Loup, Rimouski, and the Gaspé coast, all the way to the [Magdalen Islands by ferry](https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Route_Verte_1) — the longest single route in the network, crossing eight administrative regions.

The Route Verte was [conceived by Vélo Québec in the late 1980s](https://www.velo.qc.ca/en/the-history-of-cycling/the-route-verte-a-provincial-treasure/), inspired by European cycling networks. The plan was presented at the World Bicycle Conference in Montréal in 1992, and in 1995 the Ministère des Transports entrusted Vélo Québec with coordinating a 4,000 km network. The [official inauguration was August 10, 2007](https://www.velo.qc.ca/en/the-history-of-cycling/the-route-verte-a-provincial-treasure/) in Québec City. The network reached 5,000 km in 2012 and now totals [5,075 km across 382 municipalities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Route_verte) — the longest cycling route network in North America. National Geographic named it the ["best biking trail in the world"](https://www.velo.qc.ca/en/the-history-of-cycling/the-route-verte-a-provincial-treasure/) in _Journeys of a Lifetime_ (May 2009).

In the Gatineau area, Route 1 follows the [Sentier des Voyageurs](/paths/sentier-des-voyageurs) along the Ottawa River, then continues northwest through the Outaouais. It connects to many of the region's major paths — the [Voie Verte Chelsea](/paths/voie-verte-chelsea), the [Cycloparc PPJ](/paths/cycloparc-ppj), and the [Sentier de la Rivière-Gatineau](/paths/sentier-de-la-riviere-gatineau).
