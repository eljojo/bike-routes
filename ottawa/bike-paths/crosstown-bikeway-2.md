A 12-kilometre east-west cycling corridor between Westboro and Vanier, cutting across Ottawa's urban core. Unlike the river and canal paths that follow waterways, this bikeway uses city streets — segregated lanes, quiet residential roads, and pathway connectors — to create a practical crosstown route. It was designated as Crosstown Bikeway #2 in the 2013 Ottawa Cycling Plan.

The centrepiece is the [Laurier Avenue segregated bike lane](https://ottawa.ca/en/parking-roads-and-travel/road-safety/cyclists/cycling-facilities/history-ottawas-first-segregated-bike-lane/overview), which opened on July 10, 2011 — Ottawa's first. Council approved funding for the full East-West Bikeway in November 2011, and in July 2013 voted to make the Laurier lanes permanent. Beechwood Avenue, at the eastern end, remains one of the last sections of the route without continuous cycling facilities, though the city has been working to close the gap.

More useful than scenic, but that's the point. This is how you get across the city on a bike without dealing with main arterials. It connects neighbourhoods that the recreational paths don't reach, and it's one of the routes that makes Ottawa work as a cycling city, not just a city with nice paths along the water.
