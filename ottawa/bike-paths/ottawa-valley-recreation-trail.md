Where the [Ottawa-Carleton Trailway](/paths/ottawa-carleton-trailway) ends in Carleton Place, this trail keeps going. Built on a former Canadian Pacific line, the [OVRT](https://www.ontariotrails.on.ca/trail/ottawa-valley-recreational-trail) stretches 296 km from Smiths Falls all the way to Mattawa — the longest rail trail in Ontario. The section from Carleton Place to Smiths Falls is about 25 km of flat crushed stone.

Heading north from Carleton Place, the trail continues through Almonte, Pakenham, Arnprior, and up the Ottawa River valley. Every 20 km or so you hit a town, which makes longer rides manageable.

Combined with the [Ottawa-Carleton Trailway](/paths/ottawa-carleton-trailway), this gives you roughly 85 km of unbroken trail from Ottawa to Smiths Falls. From there, the Cataraqui Trail continues to Kingston, making it possible to ride across eastern Ontario almost entirely on trails.

A good overnight destination. Smiths Falls has food and accommodation, and the Rideau Canal locks nearby are worth seeing on their own.
