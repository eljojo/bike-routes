The continuation of the Trans Canada Trail beyond Carleton Place, heading southwest toward Smiths Falls. Built on a former Canadian Pacific Railway line, the [Ottawa Valley Recreational Trail](https://www.ontariotrails.on.ca/trail/ottawa-valley-recreational-trail) (OVRT) stretches 296 km from Smiths Falls all the way to Mattawa — making it the longest rail trail in Ontario. This section covers roughly 25 km of flat, crushed-stone surface between Carleton Place and Smiths Falls.

The full OVRT is managed jointly by Lanark County, Renfrew County, and the Township of Papineau-Cameron. Heading north from Carleton Place, it continues through Almonte, Pakenham, Arnprior, and up the Ottawa River valley. Every 20 km or so you hit a town, which makes longer rides manageable.

Combined with the Ottawa-Carleton Trailway, this gives you roughly 85 km of unbroken trail from Ottawa to Smiths Falls. From there, the Cataraqui Trail continues to Kingston, making it possible to ride across eastern Ontario almost entirely on trails.

A good overnight destination. Smiths Falls has food and accommodation, and the Rideau Canal locks nearby are worth seeing on their own.
