Follows Sawmill Creek through Ottawa's south end, tucked into a green corridor that feels removed from the surrounding neighbourhoods.

The creek itself is an 11-kilometre tributary of the Rideau River, rising in wetlands near Blossom Park in the Greenbelt and flowing north to meet the Rideau at Billings Bridge. It's named after a sawmill built by [Braddish Billings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sawmill_Creek), one of the area's earliest settlers. The path runs alongside the creek through wetland and past the Airport Parkway, connecting to the Brookfield Path at the north end and running south to Hunt Club Road. A newer section from Walkley to Brookfield [opened in 2014](https://bikeottawa.ca/2014/06/13/new-stretch-of-pathway-open-along-airport-parkway/).

The creek-side setting gives it a different character from the river paths — more intimate, more sheltered. It connects to the [Rideau River paths](/paths/rideau-river-eastern-pathway) and is a useful way home from longer rides out of the city.
