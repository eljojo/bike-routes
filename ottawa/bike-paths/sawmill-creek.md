A pathway following Sawmill Creek through Ottawa's south end. Paved and tucked into a green corridor that feels removed from the surrounding neighbourhoods.

Sawmill Creek itself is an 11-kilometre tributary of the Rideau River, rising in wetlands near Blossom Park in the Greenbelt and flowing north to meet the Rideau at Billings Bridge. The creek is named after a sawmill built by [Braddish Billings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sawmill_Creek), one of the area's earliest settlers. The pathway runs alongside it through the Sawmill Creek Constructed Wetland and past the Airport Parkway, connecting to the Brookfield Path at its northern end and running south as far as Hunt Club Road. A northern extension from Walkley to Brookfield, built by the City of Ottawa, [opened in June 2014](https://bikeottawa.ca/2014/06/13/new-stretch-of-pathway-open-along-airport-parkway/) — routed close to the Airport Parkway because it was the flattest alignment and avoided blasting rock or disturbing the natural environment further from the road.

The creek-side setting gives it a different character from the river paths — more intimate, more sheltered. It connects to the Rideau River pathways and provides a useful link for rides heading south or returning from longer trips out of the city.
