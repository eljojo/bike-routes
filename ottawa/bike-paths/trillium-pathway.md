A perfectly flat, lit, paved pathway running alongside the O-Train Trillium Line (Line 2) corridor through Little Italy. Three metres wide with excellent smoothness — one of the best-maintained urban cycling paths in Ottawa.

The Trillium Pathway follows the east side of the O-Train rail cut, running from the Kichi Zibi Mikan (Ottawa River Parkway) south to the Dow's Lake area near Carling Avenue. Built in phases starting in 2013, the final section connecting Carling Avenue to Dow's Lake was completed in 2018. The zero-percent grade makes it accessible to everyone, and the lighting means it works after dark.

At its north end, the pathway connects to the Ottawa River pathways and the Chief William Commanda Bridge to Gatineau. At the south end, it meets the Rideau Canal pathways and connects to the Experimental Farm area, making it a natural part of longer rides that loop through the city's core. Operated by the City of Ottawa.
