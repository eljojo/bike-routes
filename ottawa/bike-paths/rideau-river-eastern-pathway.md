This pathway follows the east bank of the Rideau River for roughly 12 km, running from Rideau Falls in the north — where the Rideau River drops into the Ottawa River — all the way south to Hog's Back Falls, where the river meets the Rideau Canal. It's part of the NCC's Capital Pathway network, paved the whole way, and quieter than the canal paths or the Ottawa River corridor — a good option when you want a ride that feels a little more removed from the city.

The route passes through New Edinburgh near its northern end, then follows Riverside Drive south past Strathcona Park and through to Billings Bridge before reaching Hog's Back Park at the southern terminus. The river is always beside you, and the tree cover is generous. In autumn, this stretch is worth riding just for the leaves. It's a green corridor that doesn't feel urban despite being well within the city — almost unknown by tourists, but well-used by residents. Note that the pathway is not lit, so plan accordingly if you're riding after dark.
