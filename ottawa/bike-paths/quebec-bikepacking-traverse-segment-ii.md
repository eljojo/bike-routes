Segment II of the [Québec Bikepacking Traverse](https://www.bikepacking.quebec/english/itineraire-qcbt/) (QcBT) — a [3,060-kilometre route from Rouyn-Noranda to the Gaspé Peninsula](https://www.bikepackadventures.ca/bikepacking-routes/quebec-bikepacking-traverse), the longest bikepacking route in Canada. Created by Etienne Théroux, the full traverse is 80% gravel, crosses 11 tourism regions and 10 national parks, with 35,900 metres of elevation gain.

This segment covers [259 km with 2,673 m of climbing](https://www.bikepacking.quebec/english/itineraire-qcbt/) through the Outaouais and Laurentides regions, from Gatineau Park northeast to Mont-Tremblant. It passes through one wildlife reserve and connects to both Ottawa and Montréal. The terrain is a mix of gravel roads, forestry roads, and trails — the kind of riding that feels remote even though you started an hour from Parliament Hill.

For riders based in the National Capital Region, this is the most accessible segment of the QcBT. You can ride into it from the [Voie Verte Chelsea](/paths/voie-verte-chelsea) or from Gatineau Park via the [Sentier du Parc de la Gatineau](/paths/sentier-du-parc-de-la-gatineau), and use it as a weekend overnighter to Blue Sea or Lac-des-Plages, or as the start of a much longer traverse east.

Note: the OSM relation for this path currently maps the full QcBT route, not just Segment II. The geometry shown on the map may extend well beyond this segment.
