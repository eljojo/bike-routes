A paved pathway winding through the Central Experimental Farm — a 427-hectare working agricultural research station right in the middle of Ottawa, operated by Agriculture and Agri-Food Canada. The farm has been here since 1886 and was [designated a National Historic Site in 1998](https://www.pc.gc.ca/apps/dfhd/page_nhs_eng.aspx?id=1818). Cycling through its fields and ornamental gardens is one of the city's quieter pleasures.

Three metres wide with good pavement, the path passes between research plots, tree collections, and open meadows. The Dominion Arboretum at the eastern edge — Canada's oldest arboretum, established in 1889 — covers 26 hectares of rolling land between Prince of Wales Drive, Dow's Lake, and the Rideau Canal, with some 1,700 species and varieties of trees and shrubs. Particularly beautiful in spring and fall. No lighting, so this is a daytime path. Maintained by the NCC.

The pathway connects to the Rideau Canal paths and the Carlington area, making it a natural part of rides that loop through Ottawa's green spaces.
