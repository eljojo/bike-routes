A path through the Central Experimental Farm — 427 hectares of working farmland, gardens, and open space right in the middle of Ottawa. The farm has been here since 1886 and was [designated a National Historic Site in 1998](https://www.pc.gc.ca/apps/dfhd/page_nhs_eng.aspx?id=1818). Cycling through its fields and ornamental gardens is one of the city's quieter pleasures.

The path passes between research plots, tree collections, and open meadows. The Dominion Arboretum at the eastern edge — Canada's oldest arboretum, established in 1889 — covers 26 hectares of rolling land between Prince of Wales Drive, Dow's Lake, and the Rideau Canal, with some 1,700 species and varieties of trees and shrubs. Particularly beautiful in spring and fall.

From here you can connect to the [Rideau Canal paths](/paths/rideau-canal) and the Carlington area — a natural part of rides that loop through Ottawa's green spaces.
